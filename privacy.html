Data Protection Mechanisms

We take the protection of Your data seriously. Here are the key data protection mechanisms we employ:

  • Data Storage: All user-generated content and sensitive data remain stored in the user's own Google Drive. Our application does not store or host this data on our servers.
  • Access Model: We use Google's OAuth 2.0 protocol for authentication and authorization. This means users grant our application specific, limited permissions to interact with their Google Drive data.
  • Data Transit: When our application needs to access or modify user data, all communication happens directly between the user's browser and Google's servers using encrypted HTTPS connections.
  • No Direct Data Handling: Our servers do not directly receive, process, or store the sensitive data from users' Google Drive accounts. We only receive access tokens that allow our application to perform authorized actions on behalf of the user.
  • Token Security: We securely store and manage the OAuth access tokens in accordance with industry best practices to prevent unauthorized access.
  • Google's Security: The data itself is protected by Google's robust security measures for Google Drive, which includes encryption at rest and in transit.
  • Compliance: We adhere to Google's API Services User Data Policy and regularly review our practices to ensure compliance with data protection regulations.
